Our Partners

Meet the founding partners who lead P Sreenivasulu and Associates with decades of combined expertise, professional qualifications, and unwavering commitment to client excellence.

CA P. Sreenivasulu

B.Com FCA ACS DISA (ICAI)

Founder Partner, P. Sreenivasulu & Associates

CA P. Sreenivasulu, the founding partner of the firm, is a seasoned professional with over two decades of experience in the field of accounting, taxation, and assurance services. A Fellow Member of ICAI and Associate Member of ICSI, he also holds a Diploma in Information System Audit (DISA), equipping him with strong expertise in both financial and technology-driven audits.

He has extensive experience in Income Tax advisory, Statutory, Internal and Concurrent Audits, and Information System Audits. His practice also covers secretarial and compliance matters for private and public companies, trusts, firms, and societies, making him a trusted advisor for diverse organizations. With his deep technical knowledge and commitment to client service, CA P. Sreenivasulu continues to guide the firm with vision and professional integrity.

CA P. Krishna Kanth

B.E. (NIT-K) ACA CISA

Partner, P. Sreenivasulu & Associates

CA P. Krishna Kanth brings a unique combination of engineering and chartered accountancy expertise to the firm. A graduate from NIT Karnataka and an Associate Member of ICAI, he is also a Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA), with specialized skills in technology-driven assurance and compliance services.

His areas of specialization include Direct & Indirect Taxation, Information System Audits, Cyber Laws, Forensic Audits, and Statutory Audits of both private and public limited companies. He has also advised extensively on ROC matters of LLPs and companies, and is recognized for his capabilities in system analysis and the design of customized ERPs and accounting applications tailored to client needs.

With a strong foundation in both technology and finance, CA Krishna Kanth plays a key role in helping clients bridge the gap between compliance, governance, and digital transformation.
